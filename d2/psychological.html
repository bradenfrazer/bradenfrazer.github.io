Psychological Effects

Audio can have a number of psychological effects on the mind. Studies have shown that it can alter a person's process of physical or emotional healing, and it can also affect one's mood.


Excerpt from "Healing at the Speed of Sound"
The above link is an excerpt from a book authored by Don Campbell and Alex Doman called "Healing at the Speed of Sound". In the few pages that are available from the 1st and 5th chapters of the book, the authors discuss the various psychological effects that audio can have on the brain. They consider what a person might hear first in their day before their eyes are opened, and how that can effect one's mood, energy level, and alertness. Many examples are also given of how sounds can effect our health, like how listening to upbeat music causes blood vessels to expand, increasing one's blood flow. Finally they also give several examples of how music can be used to manage pain, including multiple stories of people with conditions in hospitals where music ultimately saved their lives.
